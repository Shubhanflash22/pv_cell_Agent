# San Diego Residential Solar PV Market Knowledge

## Net Energy Metering (NEM)

San Diego falls under SDG&E (San Diego Gas & Electric) territory. As of 2024,
new residential solar installations are subject to NEM 3.0 (Net Billing Tariff).

Key changes under NEM 3.0:
- Export credits are significantly reduced compared to NEM 2.0.
- Export compensation is based on the "avoided cost calculator" value, which
  varies by time of day and month.
- Average export credit is roughly $0.05–$0.08/kWh (compared to ~$0.30+/kWh
  under NEM 2.0 at retail rate).
- Self-consumption is now much more valuable than exporting.

## Solar Resource

San Diego has excellent solar irradiance:
- Average peak sun hours: 5.3 – 5.7 hours/day depending on micro-climate.
- Coastal areas (La Jolla, Pacific Beach): ~5.2–5.4 h/day due to marine layer.
- Inland areas (El Cajon, Escondido): ~5.5–5.8 h/day.
- Annual insolation: ~1,900–2,100 kWh/kWp (after derate).

## Installed Costs (2024–2025)

Typical residential installed costs in San Diego:
- Before incentives: $2.80 – $3.50 per watt DC
- Average: ~$3.00/W for a quality Tier-1 panel installation.
- Smaller systems (<5 kW) tend toward the higher end ($3.20–$3.50/W).
- Larger systems (8–12 kW) can achieve $2.60–$2.90/W.
- Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC): 30% through 2032.
- After ITC, effective cost: ~$2.00–$2.45/W.

## Typical Panel Specifications

- Standard residential panel: 380–420 Wp
- Most common: 400 Wp panels (e.g., REC Alpha, Q.Peak, LG NeON)
- Panel dimensions: ~1.7m × 1.0m (~17.5 sqft)
- Module efficiency: 20–22%

## Electricity Rates (SDG&E)

SDG&E has some of the highest electricity rates in the US:
- Residential average: $0.33–$0.38/kWh (2024)
- Time-of-Use (TOU) rates:
  - Off-peak: ~$0.25/kWh
  - On-peak (4–9 PM): ~$0.55/kWh
  - Super off-peak: ~$0.20/kWh
- High rates make solar financially attractive even under NEM 3.0.

## System Sizing Guidance

- A typical San Diego home uses 7,000–10,000 kWh/year.
- 70% offset is often the sweet spot for NEM 3.0:
  - Minimizes grid dependency while limiting low-value exports.
  - Typical system size: 4–7 kW DC.
- 100% offset may lead to significant excess production exported at
  low NEM 3.0 rates — often not economical without battery storage.
- Battery storage (e.g., Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ) recommended for:
  - High nighttime load households
  - TOU rate arbitrage
  - Backup power during PSPS events

## Payback Periods

Typical payback for San Diego residential solar:
- Without battery: 6–9 years (after ITC)
- With battery: 10–14 years
- 25-year ROI: 150%–300% depending on system size and rate escalation.

## Degradation

- Industry standard: 0.5% annual degradation
- Year 25 production: ~88% of Year 1
- Most manufacturer warranties guarantee 85–87% at 25 years.
